# Algorithms taxonomy rationale

## Diagnostic granularity

Each item has one primary skill atom so an observed error can be assigned to a concrete skill. Secondary skill atoms may support an item but cannot replace the primary target.

## Block before comparison

Teach foundational mechanisms in coherent mental-unit batches before using interleaving or contrasts to discriminate among similar mechanisms.

## Similarity-driven contrasts

A contrast is warranted when learners can plausibly confuse two mechanisms and need an explicit decision boundary. The taxonomy therefore contains the five specified contrast nodes.

## Retrieval and reasoning

Prefer strategy choice, next-step prediction, solution comparison, mistake diagnosis, complexity justification, boundary reasoning, and transfer over recall of names.

## No keyword classification

Do not teach `subarray → sliding window`, `sorted → binary search`, `pair → two pointers`, or `top k → heap` as unconditional rules. Every pattern signal needs a correctness condition.
